MileTrack Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 12, 2026 · App: MileTrack (app.lovable.miletrack)

MileTrack is a personal mileage tracker. In plain terms: your data stays on your phone. The app has no user accounts, does not upload your trips to any server, does not use Google Maps or any paid API, and does not share your data with anyone.

1. What data the app collects

MileTrack only stores data you generate by using it, and it stores that data locally on your device:

  • Precise GPS location (latitude, longitude, timestamp, speed) — only while a trip is actively being recorded or automatic trip detection is enabled.
  • Trip records — start/end time, distance, duration, average and max speed, the recorded route, category (business / personal), and any note you add.
  • Fuel entries — date, gallons, price, station name, odometer, and an optional receipt photo, only when you manually add them.
  • Settings — distance unit, mileage rate, thresholds, theme, and opt-in preferences.

MileTrack does not collect your name, email, phone number, contacts, device identifiers, advertising ID, installed apps, or any usage analytics. There is no sign-up and no sign-in.

2. Why location permission is needed

  • Foreground location — to record the GPS points that form a trip.
  • Background location — so a trip you have started keeps recording accurately after your screen turns off or you switch to another app. It is used only while a trip is actively being tracked or automatic detection is enabled.
  • Foreground service — Android requires a foreground service (shown as a persistent notification) to run continuous GPS reliably. The notification tells you exactly when tracking is active.
  • Notifications — to display the tracking notification and to prompt you when a trip appears to have ended.

Location comes from Android's native OS location service. The app does not use the Google Maps SDK, Google Maps JavaScript API, or the Google Geocoding API.

Background location — prominent disclosure

Background location is requested only for one purpose: continuous mileage recording. If you are not recording a trip and automatic detection is off, the app does not access location. You can revoke background location at any time in Android Settings → Apps → MileTrack → Permissions, and the app will still work for manually entered trips.

3. How your data is stored

  • All data is stored locally on your device in the app's private storage (IndexedDB and preferences).
  • Uninstalling the app deletes your data.
  • Android auto-backup to Google Drive is disabled for this app (allowBackup="false"), so your GPS history and receipts are not copied to your Google account.
  • You can export your data yourself as a JSON backup file or a PDF/CSV report from the Settings screen. Where you send that file is entirely your choice; MileTrack is not involved.

4. Data leaving your device

MileTrack has no cloud sync, no analytics, no advertising SDKs, no Firebase, no crash reporting SDK, and no Google Maps or Google Cloud APIs. The app makes network requests in only these situations:

  1. Reverse geocoding — OFF BY DEFAULT. If, and only if, you turn on Settings → Look up trip addresses, the app will send the coordinates of a trip's start and end points to OpenStreetMap Nominatim (free, no API key) to translate them into a street address. Only those two coordinate pairs per trip are sent — never your full route. This can be turned off at any time.
  2. Nothing else. Trip routes, fuel entries, receipts, exports, and settings are never transmitted anywhere.

5. Data sharing

MileTrack does not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties. There is no advertising network, no analytics provider, and no data broker involved.

6. Your control over your data

  • Export — Settings → Export creates a JSON or PDF/CSV file you can save or share yourself.
  • Delete a single trip or entry — delete it from within the app.
  • Delete everything — use Settings → "Delete all local data", uninstall MileTrack, or clear the app's storage in Android Settings → Apps → MileTrack → Storage → Clear data.

Because there is no server-side data, there is no account to close and nothing for us to delete on your behalf.

7. Children

MileTrack is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from anyone. Because the app collects no personal identifiers, we have no way to know a user's age.

8. Security

  • Data is kept in the app's private on-device storage.
  • The Android release build has WebView debugging and Android auto-backup disabled.
  • Outbound connections are restricted by an in-app content security policy to only the optional Nominatim endpoint above.
  • Because there is no account and no cloud sync, there is no server for attackers to breach.

9. Changes to this policy

If we change how data is handled, this page and the "Last updated" date at the top will be updated. Meaningful changes will be highlighted in the app's release notes.
